An ICT Education Initiative in Africa

NTHU Africa Team

My Position: Captain of Team 2024

About Us

Since 2008 and 2015 respectively, the NTHU International Volunteer Team has been promoting information and communications technology(ICT) education programs in Tanzania and Kenya. Over the past decade, they have delivered more than 1,800 refurbished computers and established over 100 computer classrooms, benefiting around 100,000 children. In 2023, the Kenya team became the first to return on-site after the pandemic. The Tanzania team, originally scheduled to resume in 2024, was merged into the Kenya program due to a red travel alert. The team expanded its services through two separate routes, A and B. Meanwhile, the Tanzania project continues remotely and is expected to resume in-person operations in 2025. In April 2024, through a referral from ASUS, the team also conducted a preliminary assessment of ICT education needs in Eswatini, sending a scouting team after the Kenya project concluded to evaluate feasibility.

What We Have Done in 2024

Kenya

Goals:

  • Expand the scale of the project in Nyeri, Central Kenya, and extend the ICT education program to Busia, the western region of Kenya.
  • In response to technological trends, introduce AI and digital literacy courses to equip children with the skills needed to connect with the world.
  • Collaborate with the local NGO (SCINOV_AFRICA) to cultivate talents and synergy in cross-border cooperation and ICT education.

Tangible Outcomes:

  • Delivered 170 refurbished computers and set up 8 computer classrooms.
  • Visited 12 schools and taught ICT courses in 9 of them, benefiting a total of 5,592 students.
  • Organized an interschool ICT symposium with participation from 19 schools, promoting educational exchange in information technology.

Eswatini

Goals:

  • Investigate the local educational environment, understand school needs, and discuss the direction of the ICT program.
  • Pilot ICT education courses to enhance students' digital literacy and collect learning data for future team reference.
  • Establish partnerships and local connections to pave the way for future teams.

Tangible Outcomes:

  • Visited 9 schools and taught computer literacy classes at 3 of them, benefiting a total of 417 students.
  • Enhanced children's digital skills and deepened the team’s understanding of the local educational environment, fostering friendship with diplomatic allies.

Tanzania

Goals:

  • Maintained relationships with local schools and partner organizations to facilitate returning next year.
  • Collaborated remotely with local schools to provide refurbished computers, teaching materials, and educational software based on their needs.

Tangible Outcomes:

  • Delivered 92 refurbished computers, along with corresponding hardware, software, and teaching materials, to 7 schools located in northern and western Tanzania.

In The Future

ICT education requires long-term dedication, and in the post-pandemic era, the key lies in 'resilience.' In 2024, despite the red travel alert in Tanzania and anti-tax protests in Kenya, we not only upheld our commitments and successfully carried out our plans, but also proactively expanded to Eswatini, one of Taiwan's diplomatic allies, bringing help to more children. In 2025, our team is set to embark once again—not only to Kenya, Tanzania, and Eswatini, but also with an advance team heading to Mozambique. We hope that in the future, we can help bridge the digital divide and ensure that children across Africa have access to the education they deserve.
