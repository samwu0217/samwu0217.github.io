About Us

Since 2008 and 2015 respectively, the NTHU International Volunteer Team has been promoting information and communications technology(ICT) education programs in Tanzania and Kenya. Over the past decade, they have delivered more than 1,800 refurbished computers and established over 100 computer classrooms, benefiting around 100,000 children. In 2023, the Kenya team became the first to return on-site after the pandemic. The Tanzania team, originally scheduled to resume in 2024, was merged into the Kenya program due to a red travel alert. The team expanded its services through two separate routes, A and B. Meanwhile, the Tanzania project continues remotely and is expected to resume in-person operations in 2025. In April 2024, through a referral from ASUS, the team also conducted a preliminary assessment of ICT education needs in Eswatini, sending a scouting team after the Kenya project concluded to evaluate feasibility.
